2026 Goals

D. Ben Knoble on 01 Jan 2026 in Blog

A short, probably sensible-only-to-me list of goals. Timeboxed.

Guiding principles:

  • Meet good people. Do good work.
  • Keep in touch with folks.
  • Focus on a goal: file distractions away for later.

Timeboxed:

  • 1 month:
    • Finish Rusty Boat (return application results; implement read-only correctness delay?)
    • Paint regular Gamorrean Guards
    • Clean up notepad, notebook
    • Outline Luddite essay
  • 3 months:
    • Finish Cabin Mixer, show to NCCJ
    • Paint remaining Weequay Pirates
    • Draft section or 2 of Luddite essay
    • Test for 2nd Dan
  • 2 months:
    • Explore verhaal in Rust
    • Paint another squad
    • Draft section or 2 of Luddite essay
  • 4 months:
    • Rewrite mosaic program
    • Paint another squad
    • Draft section or 2 of Luddite essay
  • 2 months:
    • Publish Luddite essay?

Generally:

  • actively look for local folks: meetups, community groups, unions, etc. (wiki: 2025)
  • write more
    • Git: collected resources and my workflow
    • Critical thinking (wiki: critique/todo)
    • write about our experience reshaping our digital lives
    • share our general personal computing expertise/experience
    • start my book on GUI applications in Racket
  • make time for board games (say, 1 big one per month? We play lots of little ones already)
  • go to USK 2–3 times/week
  • keep writing fun, useful programs.
    • think about where to direct open-source energy
  • decrease household food waste

Areas I want to learn more about this year:

  • Linux
    • Keyboard configuration
    • Productivity tools: controlling Firefox tabs, dunst/rofi/etc., manipulating my monitor’s settings, …
    • Email client (aerc) for open source work
    • Setup LSP for ebuilds
    • Networking (wiki: linux-concepts/tcp; Rootless pings claims you need root for ping? FasterThanLime has a DIY ping series if memory serves)
  • Alternative technology
    • Euro Stack
    • sourcehut
    • Email server, domain, etc. (wiki: 2025)
    • VPN?
    • VPS or colocated hosting if needed?
    • Browsers (wiki: linux) if Firefox truly gets unbearable?

Political ways I can get involved:

  • book access and general literacy
  • digital/mathematical literacy
  • LGBTQIA+ support
  • local reporting
  • digital control/privacy; personal computing
  • de-toxifying masculinity
